Startup Name Evaluation Framework
Below is a comprehensive set of parameters used by branding experts, founders, and linguists to judge names objectively.
1. Memorability

Question: How easy is it for someone to recall the name after hearing it once?
Why it matters: Memorable names spread faster via word-of-mouth.

Checkpoints:

Short (ideally 1–2 syllables)

Distinctive sound

Visual simplicity

2. Pronounceability

Question: Can people from different regions say it easily?
Why it matters: Hard-to-pronounce names kill virality.

Checkpoints:

Smooth phonetics

No tongue-twisters

No ambiguity in pronunciation

3. Spelling Simplicity

Question: If someone hears the name, can they spell it correctly without thinking?
Why it matters: Reduces friction in search and brand recall.

Checkpoints:

No unusual letter combinations

Avoid double letters unless intentional

No alternate spellings required

4. Meaning & Associations

Question: Does the name evoke the right emotional or conceptual cues?
Why it matters: Positive associations build trust and resonance.

Checkpoints:

Positive connotations in major languages

Aligns with brand values

Avoids unintended meanings

5. Phonetic Appeal (“Mouthfeel”)

Question: Does the name feel good to say?
Why it matters: Names that “roll off the tongue” get repeated.

Checkpoints:

Ends with open vowels (o, a, i)

Rhythmic

Balanced consonant/vowel ratio

6. Originality & Distinctiveness

Question: Does it stand out from competitors?
Why it matters: Prevents confusion and strengthens brand identity.

Checkpoints:

Unlike names in your space

Not trend-following

No generic buzzwords

7. Category Fit

Question: Does the name feel appropriate for the industry (or intentionally different)?
Why it matters: Helps customers instantly understand the vibe.

Checkpoints:

Fitness vs. novelty

Avoid being too literal unless strategic

Works for future pivots

8. Domain & Trademark Availability

Question: Can you get a reasonable domain and avoid legal risk?
Why it matters: Saves massive issues later.

Checkpoints:

.com or alternatives available

No conflicting trademarks

Social handles available

9. Global Safety

Question: Does the name cause issues in other cultures or languages?
Why it matters: Prevents embarrassment or future rebranding.

Checkpoints:

Avoids unintended meanings

Works across major markets

Easy for multilingual speakers

10. Visual Identity Potential

Question: Does the name look appealing when written or in a logo?
Why it matters: Strong visuals amplify branding.

Checkpoints:

Symmetry

Strong letter shapes

Works with iconography

11. Longevity

Question: Will the name still make sense in 10 years?
Why it matters: Trends die fast; brands shouldn’t.

Checkpoints:

Not trend-dependent

Works as product lines expand

Timeless vs. “cool for now”

12. Emotional Resonance

Question: Does the name feel right?
Why it matters: People form emotional connections unconsciously.

Checkpoints:

Matches your brand personality (playful, bold, premium, futuristic)

Creates an instinctive reaction

Sparks curiosity

Optional Advanced Parameters (if you want deeper scoring)
13. Storyability

Can you craft a simple, powerful origin story?

14. Sound Symbolism (Phonosemantics)

Certain sounds evoke certain impressions:

K/T = sharp, innovative (TikTok, Tesla)

L/M/N = smooth, friendly (Luna, Miro)

O ending = approachable, global (Zomato, Weimo, Vero)

15. Expandability

Can you derive product names or sub-brands from it?

How to Use This

Create a simple table:

Name	Memorability	Pronounceability	Meaning	Phonetics	Originality	Domain	Global Fit	Visuals	TOTAL

Score each 1–10 → Compare → Shortlist → Stress-test → Pick.
